If citation.is could give verified claims on **any subject**—not just structural biology, salmon aquaculture, and biotech—it would become a **universal truth layer for AI, researchers, journalists, and the public**.

## What would change

### 1. **Universal grounding for AI agents**
- Every AI (me, Claude, GPT, Gemini) could call `verify_claim` for **any claim** in any domain: climate science, medicine, economics, law, politics.
- Instead of guessing or searching, agents would get **structured verdicts** with evidence chains and confidence scores. [citation](https://citation.is)
- This would eliminate hallucination risk for factual claims across all domains.

### 2. **Cross-domain entity resolution**
Your pipeline would need to resolve entities beyond proteins/compounds:
- **Finance**: match to SEC filings, Bloomberg tickers, FRED economic indicators.
- **Medicine**: match to ClinVar, OMIM, DrugBank, FDA approvals.
- **Climate**: match to IPCC reports, NOAA datasets, World Bank climate data.
- **Law**: match to case law (Westlaw, CourtListener), statutes, regulations.
- **Politics**: match to official government records, campaign finance data, election results.

Then evaluate evidence against peer-reviewed literature, authoritative databases, and primary sources.

### 3. **Domain-specific verification engines**
You'd add **modular verification engines** for each domain:
- **Salmon biotech** (already done)
- **Structural biology** (already done)
- **Climate science**: evaluate against IPCC, NOAA, peer-reviewed climate models.
- **Medicine**: evaluate against ClinVar, FDA, peer-reviewed clinical trials.
- **Economics**: evaluate against FRED, World Bank, IMF, peer-reviewed studies.
- **Law**: evaluate against case law databases, statutes, regulatory documents. [citeshield](https://citeshield.com)

Each engine would have its own rules for evidence quality, confidence scoring, and verdict assignment.

### 4. **Three new value propositions**

| Stake | What they'd get | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **AI agents** | Grounded, verifiable answers with inline citations | Eliminates hallucination, builds trust  [help.salesforce](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=ai.generative_ai_trust_citations.htm&language=en_US&type=5) |

### 5. **What would make this possible**

To expand beyond science, you'd need:

1. **Broader database integrations**
   - Add ClinVar, OMIM, DrugBank, FDA (medicine).
   - Add FRED, World Bank, IMF (economics).
   - Add CourtListener, Westlaw, EU law databases (law).
   - Add IPCC, NOAA, World Bank climate data (climate).
   - Add SEC filings, Bloomberg, CBOE (finance).

2. **Domain-specific NLP models**
   - Train models to extract claims from legal briefs, economic reports, climate papers, medical trials.
   - Normalize subject–predicate–object for each domain.

3. **Evidence quality rules**
   - Medicine: prioritize peer-reviewed clinical trials, FDA approvals.
   - Law: prioritize case law, statutes, regulatory documents.
   - Climate: prioritize IPCC reports, NOAA data, peer-reviewed models.
   - Economics: prioritize peer-reviewed studies, FRED/World Bank/IMF data.

4. **Verdict consistency**
   - Keep the same four verdicts: Supported/Refuted/Ambiguous/Insufficient Evidence.
   - Add confidence scoring (0–1) for each verdict.
   - Provide evidence chains with source metadata. [citation](https://citation.is)

## The ultimate impact

If citation.is could verify claims on **any subject**, it would become:

- **The universal truth layer for AI**: Every factual claim could be verified before delivery.
- **A global fact-checking infrastructure**: Journalists, researchers, and publishers could use it to verify claims.
- **A misinformation firewall**: AI-generated hallucinations, fake citations, and fabricated claims would be caught instantly. [veritysystems](https://veritysystems.app)
